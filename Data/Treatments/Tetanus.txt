Medications

1)Antitoxin therapy is used to target toxins that have not yet attacked nerve tissues. This treatment, called passive immunization, is a human antibody to the toxin.
2)Sedatives that slow the function of the nervous system can help control muscle spasms.
3)Vaccination with one of the standard tetanus vaccinations helps your immune system fight the toxins.
4)Antibiotics, given either orally or by injection, may help fight tetanus bacteria.
)Other drugs: Other medications might be used to regulate involuntary muscle activity, such as your heartbeat and breathing. Morphine might be used for this purpose as well as for sedation.
